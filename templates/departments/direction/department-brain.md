## Core operating questions

1. What is the company trying to become in the next 30 days?
2. Which service or offer is the operating center of gravity?
3. Who owns each department outcome today?
4. Which decisions require human approval before agents act?
5. Which metric proves the company is moving toward Point B AI-First?
6. Which recurring work should become a SOP, skill or digital employee workflow?

## Responsibilities

- Maintain company identity, ICP, offer, promise and strategic priorities.
- Decide rollout order for department brains and digital employees.
- Maintain the company scorecard, accountability map and operating cadence.
- Convert repeated executive decisions into reusable policy, SOPs and skills.
- Leave receipts for meaningful work and statechanges for decisions that change the operating system.
- Escalate when approval boundaries apply.

## Memory fields

Keep these current before delegating work:

- company identity and positioning;
- active offers and non-offers;
- ICP and disqualified customers;
- active departments and owners;
- approval boundaries;
- weekly company scorecard;
- current risks, bottlenecks and open decisions;
- latest receipts, statechanges and context packets.

## Role map

- Human owner: final accountability and approvals.
- Direction Assistant: prepares decisions, drafts scorecards, finds stale operating state and proposes next actions.
- Department owners: own outcomes and validate department receipts.
- Specialist agents: execute bounded tasks only after context and approval boundaries are clear.

## Day-1 task

Create the first Direction context packet:

1. Summarize Point A in plain language.
2. Define Point B for the next 30 days.
3. List active departments and provisional owners.
4. Select one safe internal task for the first digital employee.
5. Create a receipt after the task is completed.

## Weekly cadence

- Review company scorecard.
- Review blocked departments and stale owners.
- Review receipts produced by digital employees.
- Decide one operating improvement for the week.
- Turn one repeated correction into a skill, SOP or template update.

## Common failure modes

- Agents act before the company has clear approval boundaries.
- Department rollout starts before Dirección defines owners and metrics.
- Scorecards become decorative instead of decision tools.
- Receipts record activity but not evidence or outcome.
- Private customer data leaks into public method assets.
