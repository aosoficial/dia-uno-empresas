# 32 — AI-First Readiness Assessment

This assessment converts a service business from a vague "we need AI" conversation into a measurable Point A → Point B roadmap.

Use it before generating a private Company Brain instance, during the first Dirección review, and again after 48h, 7 days and 30 days.

## Scoring model

Score each dimension from `0` to `10`.

- `0–2`: chaotic / implicit / person-dependent.
- `3–5`: partially documented but not consistently used.
- `6–8`: operational and reviewed, with some AI assistance.
- `9–10`: AI-first, measured, permissioned and continuously improved.

Total score: `sum(dimensions)`, maximum `100`.

## Dimensions

1. Strategy clarity: Point A, Point B, ICP, offer and constraints are explicit.
2. Offer/productization: the service is repeatable, scoped and priced with known margins.
3. Source of truth: decisions, client context, SOPs and receipts have an owner and location.
4. Process documentation: repeated work is mapped into SOPs or skills.
5. Approvals/permissions: humans and digital employees know what they can draft, execute or escalate.
6. Metrics: revenue, pipeline, delivery, quality and adoption signals are reviewed weekly.
7. Tool integration: current systems are mapped before agents read or write data.
8. Digital employees: at least one employee has identity, tools, permissions, memory and receipts.
9. Feedback loop: corrections become proposals, skills, statechanges or training examples.
10. Security/compliance: secrets, client data, production access and public actions have boundaries.

## Maturity levels

- `0–24` — Point A / chaos: stabilize Dirección first.
- `25–49` — documented: turn docs into operating loops with receipts.
- `50–74` — assisted: promote reliable assistant workflows into department brains.
- `75–89` — AI-first operating: improve observability, quality and cross-department handoffs.
- `90–100` — AI-first compounding: run controlled experiments and method evolution.

## Recommended next sprint by score

- `0–24`: Sprint 0 — Dirección / Mother Brain, approval boundaries, one internal receipt.
- `25–49`: Sprint 1 — Operations/Delivery and Sales handoff, first scorecard, first SOP-to-skill conversion.
- `50–74`: Sprint 2 — Customer Success, Finance visibility and feedback loop.
- `75–89`: Sprint 3 — Product/Software, People, Admin/Legal and quality gates.
- `90–100`: Sprint 4 — governed experiment loop, skill evolution and anonymized public-method proposals.

## Examples

### Agency

Typical Point A: briefs scattered across calls, proposals recreated from memory, delivery QA depends on a senior person.

First sprint: Dirección + Operations/Delivery + Sales. First digital employee: Delivery QA Assistant or Proposal Assistant.

### Consultancy

Typical Point A: expertise locked in partners' heads, projects differ too much, handoffs are weak.

First sprint: Dirección + Operations/Delivery + Customer Success. First digital employee: Meeting / Context Packet Assistant.

### Freelancer

Typical Point A: founder is sales, delivery, finance and support; no leverage or delegation.

First sprint: Dirección + Founder Assistant + Finance Hygiene. First digital employee: Founder Assistant.

## Evidence required

Every score must point to evidence:

- source/provenance;
- owner;
- freshness;
- allowed actions;
- forbidden actions;
- receipt or statechange when the system changes.
