# Guided Pilot Delivery Model

This delivery model turns DIA UNO Empresas into a guided pilot product. The operator does not improvise; they follow sessions, evidence gates and approval boundaries until the company reaches a verified Punto B slice.

## Pilot structure

### Sprint 0 — Intake and safety

Outcome: the company has a clear operating contract.

Evidence:
- service-business intake;
- current tools map;
- approval matrix;
- first bottleneck;
- owner and freshness.

Approval: no external action. No secrets in shared materials.

### Sprint 1 — Direction / Mother Brain

Outcome: Direction owns priorities, memory, approvals, roadmap and scorecard.

Evidence:
- `company-brain.md` filled;
- `approval-boundaries.md` filled;
- first scorecard line;
- installation Receipt.

Next action: pick one department to install.

### Sprint 2 — First department

Outcome: one department can receive work through context packets and produce receipts.

Evidence:
- department brain;
- digital employee permissions;
- onboarding checklist;
- skill template selected;
- first handoff.

### Sprint 3 — First digital employee loop

Outcome: one digital employee completes a safe internal task under human review.

Evidence:
- task input;
- draft or analysis;
- human review;
- Receipt;
- scorecard update.

Approval: draft-only unless explicitly approved.

### Sprint 4 — Feedback loop and Punto B review

Outcome: the company can learn from corrections and choose the next sprint.

Evidence:
- human correction or approval;
- method improvement proposal when reusable;
- StateChange if operating state changed;
- Point B readiness scorecard.

## Operator roles

- Pilot lead: owns session pacing and safety.
- Company owner: approves priorities and boundaries.
- Department owner: owns the first workflow.
- Digital employee: drafts, analyzes and prepares within scope.

## Closeout rule

The pilot closes only when validation passes, evidence exists and the next sprint is explicit. If validation fails, write a blocker Receipt and do not claim Punto B.
