# 34 — Core Workflows A→B

These workflows show the operating change from manual service business to AI-First service business. Every workflow requires a human owner, a digital employee, metrics, receipts and approval boundaries.

## 1. Sales workflow

- Point A symptoms: leads live in messages, proposals are rebuilt from memory, pricing changes without trace.
- Point B behavior: leads become qualified context packets; proposals are drafted from approved offer rules; decisions create receipts.
- Required inputs: ICP, offer, pricing guardrails, prior proposal examples, CRM/source of truth.
- Human owner: Sales lead or founder.
- Digital employee: Sales Assistant or Proposal Assistant.
- Receipts: qualification receipt, proposal draft receipt, price approval receipt.
- Metrics: leads, calls, proposals, close rate, sales cycle, revenue booked.
- Approval boundaries: ask before sending, discounting, changing price, promising delivery dates or signing contracts.

## 2. Delivery workflow

- Point A symptoms: scope drift, QA depends on memory, client context is scattered.
- Point B behavior: delivery has an onboarding packet, active checklist, QA review and handoff receipt.
- Required inputs: signed scope, client context, delivery SOP, quality bar, risk register.
- Human owner: Delivery lead.
- Digital employee: Delivery QA Assistant.
- Receipts: onboarding receipt, QA receipt, scope-change statechange.
- Metrics: on-time delivery, rework, margin, blockers, client satisfaction.
- Approval boundaries: ask before client-visible changes, production changes, scope changes or sensitive-data use.

## 3. Customer Success / renewal workflow

- Point A symptoms: renewals are reactive; issues are handled ad hoc; feedback is lost.
- Point B behavior: recurring health review creates risks, opportunities and support actions with evidence.
- Required inputs: client goals, usage/delivery signals, feedback, renewal dates, open issues.
- Human owner: CS lead or account owner.
- Digital employee: Client Success Assistant.
- Receipts: health review receipt, renewal opportunity receipt, issue-to-SOP proposal.
- Metrics: retention, renewal rate, NPS/CSAT, unresolved issues, upsell opportunities.
- Approval boundaries: ask before messaging clients, changing commitments, offering credits or escalating legal/commercial topics.

## 4. Finance hygiene workflow

- Point A symptoms: cashflow is checked late; margin is guessed; invoices depend on founder memory.
- Point B behavior: weekly finance hygiene review flags missing invoices, margin risks and cash timing.
- Required inputs: invoice list, payment status, cost assumptions, delivery hours, revenue forecast.
- Human owner: Finance owner or founder.
- Digital employee: Finance Hygiene Assistant.
- Receipts: weekly finance receipt, invoice exception receipt, margin risk statechange.
- Metrics: cash in/out, AR, margin, overdue invoices, capacity cost.
- Approval boundaries: ask before sending invoices, changing payment terms, contacting clients or moving money.

## 5. Decision / StateChange workflow

- Point A symptoms: decisions happen in chat and disappear.
- Point B behavior: meaningful changes are captured as StateChanges with owner, source, freshness and allowed/forbidden actions.
- Required inputs: decision, reason, source/provenance, affected systems, expected outcome.
- Human owner: decision owner.
- Digital employee: Founder Assistant or Meeting / Context Packet Assistant.
- Receipts: StateChange and validation receipt.
- Metrics: decisions documented, stale decisions, reversals, approval misses.
- Approval boundaries: ask before irreversible, public, legal, economic, production or sensitive actions.

## 6. Method improvement workflow

- Point A symptoms: corrections repeat and never become system improvements.
- Point B behavior: correction → proposal → contract → implementation → validation → receipt → statechange.
- Required inputs: correction, failed expectation, proposed method change, test case, risk tier.
- Human owner: method owner.
- Digital employee: SOP Builder or Skill Evolution reviewer.
- Receipts: change proposal, validation receipt, method statechange.
- Metrics: repeated corrections, accepted improvements, rejected proposals, validation pass rate.
- Approval boundaries: public method changes require anonymization, anti-secret review and explicit approval.
